Research & Publications

Handcrafted AI: Designing Virtual Hardware for Hand Gesture-Based Interaction

This paper introduced a virtual hardware simulation layer for AI-powered hand gesture systems, enabling seamless IoT integration without physical hardware during prototyping. The gesture recognition pipeline (Mediapipe + OpenCV) achieved 93% accuracy across five gestures. Results showed that virtual hardware reduces prototyping cost and time, advancing accessibility for AI-driven IoT solutions in education and smart homes.

📄 View Paper

Smart Interaction: Combining Special Gestures, Virtual Calculations, and IoT

This research proposed a hybrid gesture + IoT framework where users perform hand movements to trigger virtual calculations and smart device control. Using CNN-based gesture recognition, the system achieved ~75ms latency in real-time. A smart-home case study demonstrated its practicality for lights, fans, and appliances. The paper highlights how gesture-driven IoT can enable inclusive technology for users with physical disabilities.
