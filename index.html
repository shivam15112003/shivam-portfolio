Research & Publications

  • Handcrafted AI: Designing Virtual Hardware for Hand Gesture-Based Interaction – Published in IEEE Xplore (ICPCT 2025).

    This paper introduced a virtual hardware simulation layer for AI-powered hand gesture systems, enabling smooth integration with IoT devices without the need for physical microcontrollers during prototyping. We designed a gesture recognition pipeline using Mediapipe and OpenCV, achieving 93% accuracy across five core hand gestures. The study demonstrated that virtual hardware emulation can significantly reduce prototyping time and improve developer accessibility, paving the way for low-cost gesture-driven AI systems for education and smart homes.

  • Smart Interaction: Combining Special Gestures, Virtual Calculations, and IoT – Published in IEEE Xplore (ICETET-SIP 2025).

    This research proposed a hybrid gesture + IoT framework where users could perform predefined hand movements to trigger virtual computations (such as arithmetic operations) and control smart devices. The system achieved real-time gesture recognition at ~75ms latency using convolutional neural networks (CNNs) combined with virtual simulation modules. A case study on gesture-based smart home control demonstrated the practicality of this approach, where lights, fans, and virtual appliances were successfully controlled through gesture inputs. The work highlights how gesture-driven IoT systems can make technology more accessible, especially for users with physical disabilities.
